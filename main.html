    Human Security
    The United Nation's human security framework is as important as the environmental, social, and governance (ESG) factors. See how children depict and describe their human security perspectives.

    SHU-YEN (COCO) Yeh, 14

    “Taiwan is quite fair in gender equality, and women are able to enjoy equal education and working opportunities. Long ago, our female ancestors made fine needlework to help the family. Those with exquisite work would earn special tattoos on their hands, which symbolize their outstanding craftsmanship and social status...”

    TABATHA Araneo, 12

    “My work represents the inequality of access to resources in the world. I sought to reflect how a few have access to much and how many have access to nothing. At the same time, for those few, the fact of being privileged by an unjust social system represents a latent threat, which is represented by the hands that lurk from the confines of the work.”

    What We Do
    The Arts Olympiad empowers students to overcome the well-documented "4th-grade slump" in their creativity. The World Children's Festival motivates the young to sever the intergenerational transmission of trauma and hatred. Child art exhibition kindle the inner child of professionals. ChildArt magazine inspires learners of all ages and develops global competencies.
